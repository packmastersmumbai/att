# Product

## Register

product

## Users

- **Security / gate staff** — standing at entry points, scanning QR codes in real time, under time pressure, often on mobile. Need instant feedback, no ambiguity.
- **Facility managers** — monitoring attendance trends, generating institutional reports, seated at a desk. Need density and clarity without cognitive load.
- **Admin / HR staff** — managing employee records, reviewing logs, configuring the system. Comfortable with data-heavy interfaces.
- **Visitors / employees** — interacting with the kiosk or receiving a QR pass on their phone. Need a frictionless, confidence-inspiring experience.

## Product Purpose

QR Att. is an institutional attendance and visitor management system. It enables real-time entry/exit tracking via QR codes, visitor pass generation, and attendance reporting for schools, offices, and facilities. Success means gate staff can process a scan in under 3 seconds, admins can pull a report in under 30 seconds, and visitors feel secure handing over their details.

## Brand Personality

Professional · Friendly · Approachable

Voice: direct, efficient, never cold. Institutional confidence without bureaucratic stiffness. Speaks to a security guard and an HR manager with equal clarity.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS cream: rounded cards, soft gradients, pastel illustrations, startup-template layouts.
- Do not look like a bootstrapped HR tool or a government legacy system.

## Design Principles

1. **Scannable at a glance** — gate staff make split-second decisions. Every status must be readable before the brain fully processes it.
2. **Density with breathing room** — admin and reports surfaces carry real data volume. Pack it without overwhelming. Stripe's information hierarchy, not a spreadsheet dump.
3. **Zero ambiguity on outcome** — scan results, visitor status, and access decisions must be unambiguous. Color, icon, and copy all reinforce the same message.
4. **Mobile is first-class, not an afterthought** — scanner and kiosk are primary mobile surfaces. Reports and visitors are used on desktop but must be fully functional on mobile.
5. **Trust through consistency** — an institutional tool earns trust by behaving predictably. Animations, spacing, and copy patterns must be coherent across pages.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. All interactive elements keyboard-accessible. Scan result states must not rely on color alone — always pair with icon and text.
